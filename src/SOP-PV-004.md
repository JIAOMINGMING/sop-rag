# 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the process for systematic screening of scientific and medical literature to identify Individual Case Safety Reports (ICSRs), safety signals, and other safety-relevant information concerning Meridian Pharma K.K. products.

# 2. Scope

This SOP applies to global literature screening for all Meridian products with a marketing authorization in any territory, and to Japanese domestic literature screening. Processing of ICSRs identified from literature is covered by SOP-PV-001; this SOP defines how such cases are identified and how Day 0 is set.

# 3. Definitions

- **Global literature databases**: Embase and MEDLINE, searched via the validated literature platform.
- **Domestic (Japanese) literature**: Journals indexed in Ichushi (医中誌) and selected Japanese society journals.
- **Hit**: A citation returned by a search strategy, prior to relevance assessment.

# 4. Responsibilities

- **Literature Screening Associate**: Executes searches, performs first-pass relevance review.
- **Second Reviewer (PV Scientist)**: Performs independent second-pass review of all excluded citations flagged as borderline and a 100% review of all included citations.
- **PV Physician**: Confirms safety relevance and signal candidacy of included articles.

# 5. Procedure

## 5.1 Search Strategy

Product-specific search strategies shall use the active substance name (INN), all Meridian brand names, and safety-relevant qualifiers. Search strategies shall be documented, version-controlled per WI-QA-004, and reviewed at least annually.

## 5.2 Screening Frequency

- Global databases (Embase, MEDLINE): searched **weekly**, on the first business day of each week.
- Japanese domestic literature (Ichushi): searched **every two weeks**.
- Ad hoc searches shall be run to support signal assessments per SOP-PV-003 and aggregate reports per SOP-PV-002.

## 5.3 Two-Level Review

The Literature Screening Associate performs first-pass review of all hits within 2 business days of search execution. The Second Reviewer independently reviews all citations marked as included and any borderline exclusions. Disagreements are adjudicated by the PV Physician.

## 5.4 ICSRs Identified from Literature

### 5.4.1 Validity and Day 0

If an abstract or article contains the four minimum criteria for a valid case (per SOP-PV-001, Section 3), an ICSR shall be created. **Day 0 is the date the search results were first reviewed by the screening staff**, not the article publication date, and not the date of full-text retrieval.

### 5.4.2 Full Text Retrieval

If the abstract suggests a valid case but lacks required details, the full text shall be ordered within **5 business days**. The case shall be processed on abstract information in the interim; the full text is handled as follow-up information per SOP-PV-001.

### 5.4.3 Translation

For Japanese-language articles containing reportable cases destined for global submission, an English summary translation shall be prepared within 5 business days of case creation.

## 5.5 Signal-Relevant Articles

Articles suggesting a new safety concern without constituting a valid ICSR (e.g., nonclinical findings, epidemiological studies, class effects) shall be forwarded to the Signal Detection Scientist within 2 business days and handled per SOP-PV-003, Section 5.2.2.

## 5.6 Missed Case Handling

If a reportable literature case is identified retrospectively (e.g., during an audit or PSUR authoring) that was present in prior search results but not actioned, this shall be recorded as a deviation per SOP-QA-001, and the case processed immediately with the original review date as Day 0.

# 6. Records

Search strategies, search execution logs, screening decisions with reviewer identity and date, and adjudication records shall be retained per WI-QA-004.
